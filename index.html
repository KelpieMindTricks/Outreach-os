Step 2 — their world
A challenge their sector faces. Something you've advised on. A trend hitting companies like theirs. Be specific.
"They're likely in a performance calibration crunch after rolling out OKRs — most EMEA industrial firms still don't have a process their leaders trust."
Step 3 — the hook
One insight sentence. Lead with what you've observed, not what you offer.
"I've been watching how [sector] is dealing with [challenge] — based on what I've seen, most firms are getting this wrong."
Don't start with "I" — lead with the sector, trend, or observation.
One sentence. If you can't, simplify the observation.
Step 4 — value offer
Something useful to share before any commercial ask. A diagnostic, checklist, or framework. Not a proposal.
"I've just put together a short diagnostic on this — happy to share it if useful."
Step 5 — the ask
Low friction. Peer-to-peer. No agenda. Make it easy to say yes.
"Would a 20-minute call be worth it? No agenda — just a peer conversation."

Offer engine
Front door offer builder
Design your low-commitment entry offer. Prove worth before the retainer conversation. The front door is what turns warm conversations into paid engagements.
A front door offer is a small, bounded piece of work that gives the client a taste of your expertise with minimal risk on their side. Think: diagnostic, audit, workshop, or mini-project. Not free — but priced to say yes easily.
Your front door offer
Use this on touch 6 of your sequence — after you've shared a credibility asset and had a discovery conversation.
The front door offer removes the barrier of a big retainer commitment. It's the first step, not the destination.
After delivery, 70%+ of front door clients move to a retainer or project. The quality of your work does the selling.

The three-tier target list
Tier 1 — Warm (start here)
Former colleagues at director/C-suite level · Past clients who saw results from your work · Trusted peers from industry networks or committees · HR leaders you've trained, mentored, or worked alongside
You need 20 names. You probably know 50 without trying.
Tier 2 — Partner referral channel
PE firms and portfolio ops directors · Large regional consulting leads who need delivery support · Employment law partners (top 10 regional firms) · Executive coaches working at C-suite level · Scale-up CEOs and CFOs from your network
One partner = multiple warm introductions. Prioritise this.
Tier 3 — New contacts (last, not first)
LinkedIn 2nd connections in target companies · People who engage with your content · Conference speakers or panellists in your space · People who commented on your former colleagues' posts
Warm them with 2–3 value touches before any direct ask.
The 8-touch connection cadence
1
Connect / reconnect
LinkedIn connection or personal email — no pitch, just a warm reconnect
2
Value share — no ask
Share something useful to them — article, insight, or observation. Nothing about you.
3
Engage their content
Like, comment meaningfully on their posts. Builds visibility and rapport.
4
Hook + insight message (Message composer)
The 5-step authority message. Leads with observation, ends with a soft peer ask.
5
Share credibility asset (Case snapshot)
Send your case snapshot or one-pager. No strings — just "thought this might be relevant."
6
Front door offer (Offer builder)
Present your low-commitment entry offer. Diagnostic, audit, or workshop before the retainer.
7
Follow-up / check-in (Follow-up generator)
Value-led check-in. No pressure. "Wanted to see how you're getting on with X."
8
Soft ask or referral
If not ready, ask who else might benefit. Keep the door open. Stay in the network.
Common mistakes to avoid
Opening with who you are instead of what you know
Positioning yourself as able to help with everything — focus on specific expertise
Generic pitches with no personalisation to name or sector
Sending one message and assuming disinterest
Starting with LinkedIn search instead of existing relationships
Pitching before you've offered any value at all
